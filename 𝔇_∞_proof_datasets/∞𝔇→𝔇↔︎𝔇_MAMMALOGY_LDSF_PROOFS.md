## Authoritative International Data Sources

### 1. PanTHERIA Database  
(*Jones et al., Ecology*)

- Life-history, ecological, and geographic traits for ~5,400 mammal species  
- Standardized variables across body size, reproduction, and ecology  
- Widely used in macroecology and comparative biology

### 2. AnAge Database  
(*Tacutu et al., Nucleic Acids Research*)

- Longevity, aging, and mortality records  
- Curated from peer-reviewed demographic studies  
- Explicit uncertainty tracking

### 3. IUCN Red List (Mammals)

- Population trends  
- Range polygons  
- Threat classification under standardized criteria  
- Global institutional review process

### 4. Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF)

- Mammalian occurrence records with spatial and temporal metadata  
- Museum specimens and vetted observation networks  
- Cross-institutional aggregation

### 5. Long-Term Ecological Monitoring Programs

- NEON (United States)  
- European LTER  
- African, Australasian, and South American mammal monitoring initiatives  

Provide replicated, population-level datasets.
