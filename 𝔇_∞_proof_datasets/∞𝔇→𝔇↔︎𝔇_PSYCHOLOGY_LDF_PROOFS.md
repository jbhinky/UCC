# PSYCHOLOGY ‚Äî LDF Empirical Proof Dataset

## Purpose
This document provides **tier-1, peer-reviewable empirical anchors** supporting the classification of **Psychology** as a **Light-Dimension Field (LDF)** under UCC.

Only the following are permitted:
- **externally measurable variables** (units + protocols),
- **replicated effects** across labs/cohorts/nations,
- **institutionally maintained datasets** (public or governed access),
- **quantified uncertainty**,
- **explicit falsifiers**.

No metaphysics, symbolism-as-causation, or narrative claims.

---

## 0) LDF Proof Standard (Non-Negotiable)
Psychology qualifies as **LDF** at a given tier if‚Äîand only if‚ÄîALL conditions hold:

1. **Measurement:** variables have units + standardized procedures.  
2. **Replication:** effects repeat across independent labs/cohorts.  
3. **Field structure:** statistical regularities persist across time/space (not identical; *stable within uncertainty*).  
4. **Uncertainty:** error bars / confidence intervals / credible intervals are reported or derivable.  
5. **Falsifiers:** clear conditions exist that would downgrade or invalidate the tier.

If any condition fails, classification must be downgraded (to LD-only, or to a lower LDF tier).

---

## 1) What ‚ÄúPsychology-as-LDF‚Äù Means Here (Operational)
> **Applied LDF claim (operational):**  
> Psychology constitutes an LDF when **measurable behavioral and cognitive structure** (accuracy, latency, error distributions, learning curves, psychometric reliability, population prevalence) exhibits **replicable statistical regularities** across individuals and contexts, and these regularities are maintained in institutional datasets.

This is *not* a claim about meaning, selfhood, or qualia. It is a claim about **measured coordination in behavior/decision/time** that persists beyond any single individual.

## 2) Tiering Constraint: LDF1‚ÄìLDF4 Only
### 2.1 LDF tiers (operational)
- **LDF1:** Individual-level task structure that replicates across labs (reaction time distributions, psychometric reliability, basic learning curves).
- **LDF2:** Group / cohort structure (age bands, clinical vs control contrasts, cross-lab cohort replication).
- **LDF3:** Population structure (national / multi-national surveys; stable prevalence + covariate structure; large cohort studies).
- **LDF4:** System coupling (measurable linkage between behavior/cognition and **external** system variables such as education outcomes, injury risk, accident rates, workforce participation, hospitalization burden‚Äî*only where the linkage is measured and replicated*).

### 2.2 Legacy alignment (LD6‚ÄìLD9 ‚Üí LDF1‚ÄìLDF4)
- **LD6 ‚âà LDF1:** replicated individual task structure  
- **LD7 ‚âà LDF2:** cohort / subgroup structure  
- **LD8 ‚âà LDF3:** population-scale psychological field structure  
- **LD9 ‚âà LDF4:** measurable coupling into large systems (health/education/economy/ecology)  

No higher claims are made here.

## 6) Substance Use: LD vs LDF (Operational, Stress-Testable)
You asked to show substance abuses as **LD and LDF collapses** of lower layers‚Äîwithout drifting into unmeasurable narrative.

### 6.1 Definitions (strictly operational)
- **LD (acute state, LD1‚ÄìLD2 compatible):** short-window measurable impairment in an individual (minutes‚Äìdays), e.g., RT slowing, increased error rate, disrupted sleep architecture, impaired inhibitory control.
- **LDF disruption (population / cohort field):** stable, measurable shifts in distributions across cohorts or populations (months‚Äìyears), e.g., prevalence structure, relapse rates, hospitalization burden, crash statistics.

### 6.2 Measurable ‚Äúcollapse‚Äù markers (allowed)

|---|---|---|

### 6.3 Dataset anchors for substance-linked field structure
- **NSDUH** provides recurring, nationally comparable distributions for use patterns and many behavioral health measures, enabling stress tests on prevalence and subgroup structure.  [oai_citation:5‚Ä°SAMHSA](https://www.samhsa.gov/data/data-we-collect/nsduh-national-survey-drug-use-and-health?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

### 6.4 Falsifiable statement (substance as LD/LDF disruption)
Psychology-as-LDF claims about substance-linked disruption **fail** if:
1) acute impairment metrics do not replicate under controlled designs, **or**  
2) population distributions do not replicate across time under institutional survey standards, **or**  
3) purported effects are not distinguishable from sampling / instrumentation noise once uncertainty is applied.

## 7) Mathematical Forms Permitted (Data-fit only; no theory-as-proof)
### 7.1 Drift / recovery time constant (restoration dynamics)
Used when longitudinal data exist (individual or cohort):
$$
x(t) = x_{base} + (x_0 - x_{base}) e^{-t/\tau_r}
$$
Where $x(t)$ is a measured variable (e.g., RT, sleep duration), and $\tau_r$ is estimated with uncertainty from data.

### 7.2 Error/latency distribution shift (field structure)
When comparing cohorts:
$$
\Delta \mu = \mu_{groupA} - \mu_{groupB}, \quad
\Delta \sigma^2 = \sigma^2_{groupA} - \sigma^2_{groupB}
$$
All differences require confidence intervals / bootstrap / Bayesian posterior intervals.

### 7.3 Reliability gate (measurement integrity)
Test‚Äìretest reliability as a minimal field-stability requirement:
$$
ICC \in [0,1]
$$
Low ICC undermines LDF claims at that measurement layer.
