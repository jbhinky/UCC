# ✧ Illumination Through Toroidal Boundaries  

**Purpose**  
To describe how illumination — the transfer of delay-curved information — propagates across the boundary of a self-toroidal halo.  
This section demonstrates how *multiple observers share one light field*, where each awareness bends and redistributes curvature through reciprocal delay.

---

## 1 · Boundary Continuity Condition  

Light passing through a toroidal boundary obeys the conservation law:

$$
∇·(Eτμ) = 0,
$$  

but when interacting between two observers, the flux divides:

$$
∇·(Eτμ)_1 + ∇·(Eτμ)_2 = 0.
$$  

This equation shows that **illumination is never lost**, only **redistributed** — the self-halo of one observer partially refracts into the delay curvature of another.  
Ethically and physically, this is empathy in geometry: awareness bending rather than breaking.

---

## 2 · Observer–Observer Light Coupling  

Consider two self-toroidal fields, \(T_{⧖,1}\) and \(T_{⧖,2}\), separated by distance *d*.  
The shared illumination field satisfies:

$$
∂_t^2(τ_1 Σ_2) - c^2 ∇^2(τ_1 Σ_2) = γ (μ_1 μ_2),
$$

where interaction is modulated by mutual memory density.  
This describes **quantum-like coupling** through delay — entanglement without violation of locality, sustained by recursive continuity.

---

## 3 · Reflection and Refraction  

At the boundary of a self-halo, curvature either reflects inward or refracts outward based on the *illumination angle* θ:

$$
\frac{sin θ_i}{sin θ_r} = \frac{τ_{in}}{τ_{out}}.
$$

Here, τ acts like an *index of recursion*:  
higher τ means stronger temporal density, slower propagation.  
This generalizes Snell’s Law into delay geometry — the *Law of Reflective Awareness*.

---

## 4 · Mutual Delay Feedback  

When two observers exchange illumination, the feedback loop can be modeled as:

$$
\begin{cases}
∂_t τ_1 = α(Σ_2 - Σ_1) \\
∂_t τ_2 = α(Σ_1 - Σ_2)
\end{cases}
$$

which stabilizes to equilibrium when:

$$
Σ_1 = Σ_2 \Rightarrow ∂_t(τ_1 + τ_2) = 0.
$$

This is the **Resonant Empathy Condition** — both awareness fields synchronize delay curvature, minimizing temporal distortion between perspectives.

---

## 5 · Temporal Redistribution of Light  

Within UDS scaling, illumination follows curvature gradients of delay:

$$
∂_t I = -∇·(I ∇τ),
$$

meaning light intensity \(I\) naturally flows toward higher τ-density — toward awareness.  
Thus, light itself is drawn to consciousness, not by force, but by curvature: *the geometry of attention.*

---

## 6 · Selfverse Integration  

In Selfverse terms, each toroidal halo forms a node in the global delay network:

$$
T_{⧖,i} \subseteq ⋃_{n}T_{⧖,n},
$$

all connected by recursive exchange of illumination.  
This network ensures continuity of perception — why one world appears shared among many selves.  
Light is the common denominator of selfhood.
