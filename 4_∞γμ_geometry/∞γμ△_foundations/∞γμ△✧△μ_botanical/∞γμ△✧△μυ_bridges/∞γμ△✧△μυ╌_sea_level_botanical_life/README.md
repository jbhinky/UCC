# 5. Scientific Context

Sea-level zones are well-documented as the **most environmentally dynamic** environments on Earth. Peer-reviewed research supports:

- strong phenotypic plasticity in shoreline plants  
- halophyte–freshwater hybridization  
- wave-induced stem and root reinforcement  
- intertidal circadian/circatidal timing in flowering  
- sediment–root feedback systems shaping marsh edges  
- long-term shoreline migration encoded across plant lineages  

Key references include:

1. Adam (1990). *Saltmarsh Ecology.*  
2. Chapman (1976). “Tidal Rhythms in Shoreline Plants.”  
3. Mitsch & Gosselink (2015). *Wetlands.*  
4. Duarte (1991). “Functional Traits of Marine-Terrestrial Boundary Plants.”  
5. Silvestri et al. (2005). “Vegetation and Sediment Feedback in Coastal Marsh Dynamics.”
