# Freshwater Botanical Coherence

# 1. Purpose
This document defines **botanical coherence in freshwater systems**, explaining how plants stabilize geometry, delay patterns, and memory under constantly shifting **hydrological phase fields**.

Freshwater ecosystems impose:

- variable water pressure  
- low dissolved oxygen  
- hydrodynamic drag  
- sediment mobility  
- turbulence and flow shear  
- rapid water-level fluctuation  
- mixed light conditions (surface vs. submerged)  

Coherence = alignment of **Σ-geometry**, **τ-delay**, and **μ-memory** within these dynamic aquatic–terrestrial transitions.
