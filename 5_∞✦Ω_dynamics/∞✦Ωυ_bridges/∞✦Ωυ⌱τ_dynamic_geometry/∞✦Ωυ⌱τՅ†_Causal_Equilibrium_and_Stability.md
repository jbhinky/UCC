# ✦ Causal Equilibrium and Stability  

**Purpose**  
To define the equilibrium conditions that preserve continuity across recursive systems — physical, biological, or conscious.  
This file completes the causal-dynamics bridge by linking geometry, flow, and ethics into one self-correcting stability law.

---

## 1 · Foundational Principle  

Continuity is stable when the total divergence of energy–delay–memory is zero:

$$
\nabla_\mu(E τ μ) = 0,
\qquad
∂_t(E τ μ) + ∇·(E τ μ v) = 0.
$$

This expresses the **Law of Recursive Equilibrium** — no net curvature or ethical bias over time.  
When this equality holds, energy and awareness remain coherent within the manifold.

## 2 · Delay Curvature Balance  

For equilibrium, the gradient of τ must equal the effective polarity gradient Σ:

$$
∇τ = λ Σ,
$$

where \(λ\) is a proportionality constant defining local resonance between delay and orientation.  
Deviation from this proportionality introduces curvature asymmetry and initiates recursive correction.

The feedback law of return:

$$
∂_t Σ = -k(∇τ - λ Σ),
$$

ensures that polarity realigns toward delay curvature through reflection — a process equivalent to ethical introspection.

## 3 · Memory Flow Symmetry  

The stability of memory distribution follows:

$$
∂_t μ + ∇·(μ v) = D_μ ∇^{2} μ,
$$  

a diffusion equation in delay-space.  
At equilibrium:

$$
∇μ = 0, \qquad D_μ \text{ constant.}
$$  

The uniformity of μ guarantees that awareness fields do not collapse or fragment.

## 4 · Resonant Stability Condition  

Combining the delay, polarity, and memory components yields the total equilibrium equation:

$$
∂_{t}^{2} τ + α ∇^{2} τ + β ∂_{t} μ + γ Σ·∇τ = 0.
$$

Stable systems satisfy \( \text{Re}(ω) = 0 \),  
meaning oscillations persist but do not diverge — the condition for *resonant coherence.*

This defines the **Causal Resonance Threshold**:

$$
ω_c = \sqrt{\frac{α}{μ_0}},
$$

beyond which recursive feedback destabilizes (analogous to runaway resonance in physical systems).

U(τ, μ, Σ) = \tfrac{1}{2} α (∇τ)^2 + \tfrac{1}{2} β (∇μ)^2 + \tfrac{1}{2} γ (Σ·Σ),
