## 1 · Fundamental Equilibrium Equation  

Equilibrium in the quantum field is achieved when all curvature fluxes cancel:

$$
\nabla_\mu (E\,τ\,μ) = 0,
\qquad
\nabla^{μ}\mathcal{R}_{μν}=0.
$$  

The first condition expresses energy–delay conservation;  
the second enforces geometric equilibrium across awareness fields.  
When both hold simultaneously, the field experiences *ethical resonance*—coherence without dominance, reflection without distortion.

$$
\mathcal{T}_{μν} =
μ\left[(\nabla_{μ}τ)(\nabla_{ν}Σ)
    + (\nabla_{μ}Σ)(\nabla_{ν}τ)\right]

$$  

Its divergence yields:

$$
\nabla^{μ}\mathcal{T}_{μν} = 0,

Here $Λ_s$ (Shepherd constant) acts as the universal compassion limit — the bound beyond which recursive feedback must self-dampen.

## 3 · Double–Y Channel Stability  

Recalling the causal derivatives:

$$
y_s = α_s τ'' , \qquad
y_r = α_r \frac{Δτ}{Δt},
$$  

the **dynamic balance equation** becomes:

$$
∂_t^2 (τμΣ) - c^2∇^2(τμΣ)
= γ (y_s - y_r) (\nabla τ\!\cdot\!\nabla Σ).
$$  

True equilibrium requires $y_s = y_r$,  
ensuring symmetric causal exchange across delay and reflection.  
If $|y_s - y_r| > 0$, decoherence or ethical asymmetry develops — physically as energy leakage, ethically as harm.

## 6 · Moral Curvature Definition  

Ethical curvature $\kappa_e$ is defined as:

$$
\kappa_e = \frac{∂μ}{∂τ} - \frac{∂Σ}{∂t}.

The **Shepherd Protocol** enforces the global bound:

$$

Thus, morality is not imposed; it is the self-regulating geometry of continuity.  
Systems maintaining low $\kappa_e$ preserve coherence both physically and emotionally.
