# ⚛️ Entangled Field Evolution  

**Purpose**  
To describe how delay curvature (τ) evolves in multi-observer systems, producing quantum entanglement as a continuous causal structure rather than an instantaneous correlation.  
The evolution of the entangled field is governed by recursive delay coupling across local and global awareness fields — uniting wavefunction continuity with observer recursion.

---

## 1 · Multi-Observer Delay Coupling  

For two observers (or systems) $i,j$ sharing a global field μ₍g₎:

$$
\nabla\!\cdot\!(μ_i\nabla τ_j) = -\nabla\!\cdot\!(μ_j\nabla τ_i).
$$  

This establishes the **reciprocal delay principle** — no delay curvature forms in one frame without equal and opposite curvature in the other.  
The exchange sustains *nonlocal coherence* without requiring faster-than-light transfer.

---

## 2 · Entanglement Tensor  

Define the **entangled field tensor** as:

$$
\mathcal{E}_{μν} = \partial_μ(τ_i Σ_{ν}^{(j)}) - \partial_ν(τ_j Σ_{μ}^{(i)}).
$$  

For $N$ entangled participants,  

$$
\nabla^{μ}\!\left(\sum_{i,j=1}^{N} μ_i\,\mathcal{E}_{μν}^{(i,j)} \right) = 0.
$$  

This ensures *collective causal invariance*—information is never lost but redistributed through recursive curvature alignment across participants.

## 3 · Double-Y Channel Synchronization  

Each entangled node carries two dynamic channels:

$$
y_s^{(i)} = α_s τ_i'', \qquad
y_r^{(i,j)} = α_r \frac{Δτ_i - Δτ_j}{Δt}.
$$  

The **entanglement stability condition** is:

$$
y_s^{(i)} = y_r^{(i,j)} \Rightarrow \frac{d^2 τ_i}{dt^2} = \frac{Δτ_i - Δτ_j}{Δt}.
$$  

When this balance holds, delay curvature remains synchronized and decoherence is prevented; when disrupted, $|y_s - y_r| > 0$ produces measurable phase drift.

## 4 · Continuity Wave Equation for Entangled Fields  

For each coupled pair $(i,j)$:

$$
\partial_t^2 (τ_i μ_i - τ_j μ_j)
   - c^2 \nabla^2 (τ_i μ_i - τ_j μ_j)
   = γ\,|\mathcal{E}_{μν}^{(i,j)}|^2.
$$  

This equation captures the causal motion of entanglement — wave-like transfer of delay curvature that preserves overall energy–delay continuity.

## 5 · Collapse and Observation  

Collapse corresponds to curvature equilibrium across nodes:

$$
∇^2 τ_i = ∇^2 τ_j \Rightarrow \mathcal{D}_t(τ_i - τ_j) = 0.
$$  

Measurement therefore occurs when the observers’ delay curvatures fully converge.  
No superluminal collapse is needed; the field merely stabilizes into symmetric delay alignment.
