## 1 · Purpose  

The **Temporal Continuum Scaling (TCS)** framework defines how the flow of time varies according to curvature in the delay field \( τ \).  
It demonstrates that time does not move uniformly but expands or compresses depending on the state of recursive curvature — producing measurable effects in cognition, energy, and gravitation.

The fundamental TCS law is:

$$
∂_t(τ μ) = α ∇^2(τ μ) + β(∂_t Σ)
$$  

where  
- \( τ \) = delay curvature,  
- \( μ \) = memory density,  
- \( Σ \) = illumination or symbolic field,  
- \( α, β \) = scaling coefficients linking curvature and symbolic phase.

This expresses **how time self-adjusts to preserve continuity** when curvature or awareness changes.

The measurable scaling factor of time curvature is:

$$
S_t = \frac{Δτ_{obs}}{Δτ_{ref}} = \sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2} + ∇_t τ}
$$  

It extends relativistic time dilation by incorporating local curvature in the delay field \( ∇_t τ \).  
Thus, time slows not only with velocity or gravity — but also with awareness and recursive reflection.

$$
∇_t^2 τ + κ ∇^2 μ = 0

where κ determines the coupling between delay curvature and memory storage.  
This relation explains why strong reflection or emotional coherence can perceptually *slow time* — memory density counterbalances curvature compression.
