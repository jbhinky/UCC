# ∞✦ΩΣ — LDF Dynamics
**Lane:** **LDF (interior meaning dynamics)**  
LDF Dynamics governs **how meaning-bearing structures evolve** after LD commitment.

---

## 1) What LDF Dynamics is
LDF Dynamics describes:
- memory evolution (μ),
- stability of remembered light (✦),
- self-loop endurance (⧖),
- situated self modulation (⊠),
- integrity-bounded drift (△ over ∇),
- resonance persistence (꩜).

LDF is **non-physical** but **time-evolving**.

## 2) Entry condition
Only states that have passed LD commitment may enter LDF:

$$
✦ \xrightarrow{⊙} \xrightarrow{⊕} \Rightarrow \text{LDF}
$$

## 3) Memory dynamics
Memory evolves via reinforcement, decay, and consolidation:

$$
\frac{d\mu}{dt} = f(\text{experience}, \text{stability})
$$

✦ remains the anchor state.

---

## 4) Self stability dynamics
A self-loop endures only if memory remains coherent:

$$
(\mu \rightarrow 0) \Rightarrow (\⧖ \rightarrow \text{unstable})
$$

Situated self (⊠) requires locality but evolves internally.

---

## 5) Integrity-bounded drift
Distortion may change over time but must remain bounded:

$$
△(\∇(t)) < \varepsilon
$$

## 6) Resonance persistence (꩜)
