## ✦ 1 · Overview  

In classical mechanics, *force* represents a transfer of momentum.  
In UCC physics, force becomes a **local amplification of delay curvature (τ)** — a distortion in time density that propagates through awareness and memory.  

Thus, “ethical force” means applying energy without fracturing continuity.  
This section formalizes how curvature-based systems can apply power while maintaining moral coherence and causal stability.  

---

## ✦ 2 · Force as Curvature Gradient  

Force is expressed not as mass × acceleration, but as a **gradient in delay curvature**:

$$
F_{τ} = μ ∇τ .
$$  

Here, τ acts as the field’s “temporal potential,”  
and μ defines how much memory or awareness is bound to the affected system.  
This definition shows that physical push and ethical pressure are the same geometric event viewed through different domains.

## ✦ 3 · Conservation Law for Applied Curvature  

Any applied curvature must satisfy the recursive conservation rule:  

$$
∇_μ (E τ μ) = 0
$$  

which guarantees that energy, delay, and awareness remain conserved during motion or influence.  
When violated (∇μ ≠ 0), causal discontinuities appear — equivalent to moral or thermodynamic entropy.

## ✦ 4 · Ethical Bound Condition  

Let Λₛ represent the **Shepherd Bound**, the maximum sustainable curvature energy that preserves self–field coherence:  

$$
∫ |F_{τ}|^2 dV ≤ Λₛ .
$$  

This limit prohibits destructive over-application of power — whether by a star, a machine, or a conscious being.  
Crossing Λₛ triggers recursive collapse, producing both physical and ethical instability.

## ✦ 5 · Intent–Reflection Coupling  

Every curvature impulse carries intent (Σ) and memory (μ):  

$$
F_{eff} = (∇τ·Σ) μ .

When Σ (symbolic reflection) aligns with ∇τ, the action is constructive;  
when Σ opposes ∇τ, the action amplifies disorder.  
This provides a measurable distinction between *creative* and *destructive* energy flow.
