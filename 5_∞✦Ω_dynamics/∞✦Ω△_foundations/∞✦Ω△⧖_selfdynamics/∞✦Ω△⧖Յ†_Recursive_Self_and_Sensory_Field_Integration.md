# ✦ Recursive Self and Sensory Field Integration  
### *How the senses become geometry — and how geometry forms the self.*

The sensory field is not external.  
It is the *boundary condition* of the self.

This file formalizes how the recursive self (⧖) integrates with the sensory field across delay (τ), symbolism (Σ), and memory (μ), producing the coherent experience of perception, identity, emotion, and continuity.

---

# 1 · The Self as a Recursive Sensory Equation

The self is the recursive stabilizer of sensory input:

$$
\⧖ = (A \cup C)[\tau + \Sigma + \mu]
$$

Where each sense contributes its own curvature:

- **visual curvature**: $$\tau_v, \Sigma_v, \mu_v $$
- **auditory curvature**: $$\tau_a, \Sigma_a, \mu_a $$
- **tactile curvature**: $$\tau_t, \Sigma_t, \mu_t $$
- **olfactory & gustatory curvature**: $$ \tau_o, \Sigma_o, \mu_o $$
- **interoceptive curvature**: $$ \tau_i, \Sigma_i, \mu_i $$

The self is the *integration surface* of these sensory curvatures.

# 2 · Sensory Delay Field (τ_s)

Each sensory channel has a different natural delay constant:

$$
\tau_s = \{\tau_v,\tau_a,\tau_t,\tau_o,\tau_i\}
$$

Vision resolves in ~100–150 ms.  
Hearing resolves in ~20–40 ms.  
Touch resolves in ~5–15 ms.  
Interoception resolves in ~250–500 ms.

Identity exists **behind real-time** because sensory delays must unify before collapse.

Thus the mind is always ≈80–150 ms *after* the world.

# 3 · Sensory Symbolism Field (Σ_s)

Σ encodes the meaning of sensory input.

For each modality:

$$
\Sigma_s = f_s(\tau_s, \mu_s)
$$

Examples:

- vision maps edges → objects → stories  
- audition maps vibrations → tone → emotion  
- touch maps pressure → warmth → safety  
- interoception maps heartbeat → emotion → intuition  

Meaning is recursive: the senses interpret one another.

# 4 · Sensory Memory Continuity (μ_s)

Memory of sensory input forms temporal continuity:

$$
\mu_s = \int_{t-\Delta t}^{t} s(\tau_s) \, dt
$$

This integrates:

- past perception  
- present sensation  
- predicted future input  

Identity stability requires:

$$
\mu_v \approx \mu_a \approx \mu_t \approx \mu_i
$$

When memory continuity diverges → fragmentation, anxiety, dissociation.

# 6 · Sensory Resonance and Emotional Geometry

Emotion is the resonance of the sensory field:

$$
E = \sum_s \left( \tau_s \Sigma_s \right)
$$

Thus emotion is:

- temporal (how long things last)  
- symbolic (what they mean)  
- sensory (how they feel)  

This is why:

- sound can make you cry  
- color can calm a traumatized mind  
- a heartbeat can synchronize two identities  

Emotion is geometry in the body.
