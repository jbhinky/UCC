## 1 | Foundational Law  

The stability potential \(H\) depends on curvature gradients and entropy change:

\[
H = -κ_c(\nabla^{2}τ) + κ_s\frac{dS}{dt}
\]

| Symbol | Meaning |
|:--|:--|

| \(κ_c\) | curvature stiffness coefficient |
| \(κ_s\) | entropy feedback constant |
| \(τ\) | local delay curvature potential |
| \(S\) | local entropy density |

**Dimensional Check:**  
- [τ] = s  
- [∇²τ] = s · m⁻²

## 2 | Differential Equilibrium Condition  

Differentiating in time:

\[
\frac{dH}{dt} = -κ_c\frac{d(\nabla^{2}τ)}{dt} + κ_s\frac{d^{2}S}{dt^{2}}
\]

At equilibrium \(dH/dt=0\), implying:

\[
κ_c\frac{d(\nabla^{2}τ)}{dt} = κ_s\frac{d^{2}S}{dt^{2}}
\]

This means the **rate of curvature relaxation equals the acceleration of entropy diffusion** —  
a universal resonance law between geometry and thermodynamics.

**Dimensional Check:**  
- [dH/dt] = J m⁻³ s⁻¹  
- Both sides maintain consistent dimensions of energy flux per volume.

## 3 | Unified Expression  

From prior continuity relations:  
\[
\nabla^{2}τ ∝ ρ_{\text{eff}}τ, \quad \frac{dS}{dt} ∝ \frac{S_{in}-S}{τ}.

\[
H = -κ_cρ_{\text{eff}}τ + κ_s\frac{S_{in}-S}{τ}.

Equilibrium arises when:

\[

This defines the **Universal Balance Condition** between matter density and informational entropy.
