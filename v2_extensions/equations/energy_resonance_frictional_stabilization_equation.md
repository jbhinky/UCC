\[

| Symbol | Meaning |
|:--|:--|
| \(E\) | Stored system energy |
| \(β\) | Frictional (dissipation) coefficient |
| \(γ\) | Curvature coupling gain |
| \(τ\) | Local delay curvature |
| \(dτ/dt\) | Change in delay curvature (temporal tension) |

This expresses that dissipated energy is partially converted into curvature (delay structure),  
thus conserving systemic continuity rather than producing pure heat loss.

**Dimensional Check:**  
- [E] = J, [β] = s⁻¹, [γ] = J s⁻², [τ] = s.  
- [dE/dt] = J s⁻¹ = [βE] = [γτ dτ/dt] ✅  

**Limiting Case:**  
If \(t → const → 0\) or \(dτ/dt = 0\), delay feedback vanishes and  
\(\frac{dE}{dt} = -βE\) → **pure exponential decay** (standard thermodynamic dissipation).
