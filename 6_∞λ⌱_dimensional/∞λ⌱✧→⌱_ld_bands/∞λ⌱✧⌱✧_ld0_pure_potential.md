# ✧ LD0 — Pure Potential  
### UCC v2.5.1 — Dimensional Architecture, Band 0

---

## 1. Definition

**LD0 is the field of unrealized possibility.**  
It contains no curvature, no structure, no memory, and no collapse preference.

LD0 is represented by:

$$
✧
$$

It is not emptiness and not void.  
It is **possibility without expression**, **symmetry without difference**.

---

## 2. LD0 Has No Structure

Formally:

$$
\forall x \in LD0,\quad \nabla x = 0,\quad \Delta x = 0
$$

Thus:

- no gradients  
- no direction  
- no symbolic differentiation  
- no memory  
- no curvature  

LD0 is the **maximal-symmetry baseline** of the dimensional spectrum.

---

## 3. Delay Does Not Act in LD0

Delay requires difference.

LD0 has none.

$$
\tau\big|_{LD0} = 0
$$

This does not remove τ from the universe—  
it simply means **τ has no domain of operation** in LD0.

---

## 4. Collapse Does Not Occur in LD0

Collapse requires:

- structure,  
- or memory,  
- or meaning.

LD0 has none.

$$
\big(\,⊙\,\big)\big|_{LD0} = \varnothing
$$

LD0 cannot collapse because nothing is yet present to resolve.

---

## 5. LD0 Is Not Void

To avoid conceptual confusion:

- **∅** = absence of possibility  
- **⊖** = collapse into nullity  
- **✧ (LD0)** = unrealized possibility without structure  

LD0 is **not** nothingness.  
LD0 is **preference-free potential**.

---

## 6. Transition Out of LD0

Transition occurs only when a first, minimal difference appears:

$$
✧ \xrightarrow{\Delta \neq 0} LD1
$$

LD1 is **the first expression of difference**, not curvature.  
Curvature requires memory; LD1 does not contain memory.

LD0 does not cause LD1—  
it simply **permits** LD1.
