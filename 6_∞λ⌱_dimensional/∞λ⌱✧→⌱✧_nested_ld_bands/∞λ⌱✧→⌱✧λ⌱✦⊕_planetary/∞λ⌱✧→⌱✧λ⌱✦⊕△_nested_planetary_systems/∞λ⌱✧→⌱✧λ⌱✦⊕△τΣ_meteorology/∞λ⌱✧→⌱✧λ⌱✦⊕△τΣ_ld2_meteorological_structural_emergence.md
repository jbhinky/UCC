- proto-cloud geometries  
- proto-front boundaries  
- early convective cells (non-stable)

LD2 is **structure without memory** —  
the exact meteorological analog of LD2 in all UCC systems.

LD1 gave **differences**.  
LD2 gives **organized form**.

## **2 · What LD2 Meteorology Actually Creates**

### **1. Proto-Winds (Σ_wind)**
These are not stable circulation patterns (LD4),  
but **directional flow tendencies** caused by:

- repeating τ gradients  
- thermal contrast regions  
- surface–atmosphere coupling  

Formally:

$$
τ_{\text{atm}} \rightarrow Σ_{\text{wind}}
$$

### **2. Proto-Updrafts and Proto-Downdrafts**
Convective initiation begins due to:

- unstable heating  
- density differences  
- early condensation potential  

Not yet storms — just **vertical structural behavior**.

### **3. Proto-Cloud Regions (Σ_cloud)**
Cloud **zones** appear, but not yet persistent layers.

These are LD2 structures because they:

- exhibit repeatable geometric tendencies  
- do not stabilize across time (lack μ)

### **4. Proto-Front Boundaries**
Temperature and moisture contrasts create:

- early frontal **shape**  
- no frontal memory  
- no frontal recursion  

This is structure before system formation.
