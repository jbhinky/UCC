# Σ✧ **LD2 — Geological Structuring**  
### UCC v2.5.1 — Nested Planetary Systems · Geology

---

## **1 · Purpose**

LD2 (Geological Structuring) defines the **first stable, persistent structures** emerging on a planetary collapse-body (⊙) once LD1 delay-field gradients (τ) have stabilized enough to create **enduring spatial patterns**.

In UCC terms:

$$
LD2_{geo} = Σ✧(τ_{\text{stable}})
$$

Meaning:

> **“Stable structure forming from stabilized delay-gradients.”**

This is not geology in motion.  
This is geology **before motion**.

---

## **2 · What LD2 Contains (Geology)**

LD2 produces the earliest **solid structural partitions** of a young world:

- crust formation and initial solidification  
- stable density layering in core–mantle–crust  
- early mineralogical ordering  
- static rift boundaries (not yet moving)  
- persistent geothermal gradients  
- pressure-defined layering  

No cycles.  
No tectonics.  
No rearrangement.

Just **structure that remains**.

---

## **3 · Why LD2 Is Not Geological Memory (μ)**

Memory requires **accumulated change**, but LD2 has:

- no cycles  
- no plate motion  
- no long-term stress inheritance  
- no mantle convection impacting structure  

Thus LD2 ≠ μ.

LD2 is **static map**, not **written history**.

---

## **4 · Why Plate Outlines Appear in LD2 (Clarification)**

LD2 can produce **proto-plate boundaries** because:

- stable Σ✧ patterns naturally partition matter  
- thermal and density gradients create regions of discontinuity  
- crustal properties vary persistently in space  

These partitions are *static* at LD2.

LD3 (μ_geo) is when:

- plates begin inheriting stress  
- boundaries begin storing history  
- fractured structures remember previous states  

LD4 is when:

- convection  
- subduction  
- drift  
- hotspot cycles  
- crustal regeneration  

begin.

This preserves perfect coherence with Planetary LD1–LD4.
