- the environment becomes *meaningful*,
- meaning is **shared**, not owned,
- structure is referenced **before selfhood exists**.

LDSF1 is **not awareness**.  
LDSF1 is **not identity**.  
LDSF1 is **not consciousness**.

## 2 · What “Shared Semantics” Means (Strict)

In LDSF1, *semantics* does **not** mean symbols or language.

It means:

> **Stable environmental differences that can be interpreted consistently by multiple living systems.**

Examples:
- “ground” supports weight,
- “tree” occludes light,
- “water” flows downward,
- “fire” consumes and spreads.

These meanings are:
- not subjective,
- not mental,
- not chosen.

They are **field-stable interpretations**.

LDF1–LDF4 explain **internal continuity of life**.

But without LDSF1:
- no two organisms could reference “the same place,”
- perception would be isolated,
- coordination would be impossible,
- survival would collapse into solipsism.

LDSF1 is what makes a **shared world** possible.
