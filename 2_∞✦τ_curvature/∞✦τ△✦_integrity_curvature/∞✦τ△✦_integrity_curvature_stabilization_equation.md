# ∞✦τ — Integrity Curvature Stabilization Equation  

## **1. Purpose**

Integrity curvature (△✦τ) is the restorative geometry of a system.  
Once recovery begins (File #3), the system must **stabilize** this integrity so it becomes persistent, self-reinforcing, and resistant to re-distortion.

Stabilization occurs when:

- Σ (interpretive equilibrium) is locked,  
- μ (memory curvature) aligns with Σ,  
- ⇴ (memory flow) becomes coherent and steady,  
- △✦τ dominates all local distortion sources,  
- Յ (pattern geometry) constrains stability into enduring form.

This file defines the **canonical stabilization law for integrity curvature**.

## **2. Stabilization Requirements**

A system stabilizes its integrity curvature only when:

### **1. Memory flow converges to interpretive equilibrium**
$$
\nabla⇴ \rightarrow Σ
$$

### **2. Restorative curvature fully dominates**
$$
△✦τ \gg \nabla✦τ
$$

### **3. Memory curvature aligns**
$$
μ' \approx Σ
$$

Alignment of Σ, μ, and ⇴ produces *curvature coherence* —  
the necessary condition for **long-term stability**.

Meaning:
