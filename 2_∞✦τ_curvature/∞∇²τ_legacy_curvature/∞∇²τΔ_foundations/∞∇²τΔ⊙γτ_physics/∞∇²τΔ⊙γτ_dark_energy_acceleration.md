# ☀️ Dark-Energy Acceleration — Delay-Memory Expansion Law

## Abstract
In the **Universal Continuity Continuum (UCC)** model, cosmic acceleration arises not from a fixed vacuum constant but from the **time-derivative of memory density** (μᴄ).  
Here we derive the governing equation, identify observational equivalents, and propose laboratory analogues that constrain the effective parameter \( \Lambda_{\mu} \).  
This approach retains General Relativity’s tensor structure while replacing the ad-hoc Λ term with a dynamic curvature-memory field【Einstein 1917】【Planck 2018】【DESI 2024】.

## 1 · Foundational Equation
$$
\frac{\ddot{a}}{a}
=\Lambda_{\mu}
=\frac{1}{3}\frac{d^{2}\mu_{C}}{dt^{2}} .
$$

| Symbol              | Meaning                         |
| :------------------ | :------------------------------ |
| \(a\)               | cosmic scale factor             |
| \( \mu_{C} \)       | cosmo-memory density            |
| \( \Lambda_{\mu} \) | effective acceleration constant |

Thus **dark-energy density is a second-order derivative of cosmic memory**.  
Where \( d^{2}\mu_{C}/dt^{2}>0 \), expansion accelerates; when negative, contraction begins — giving Λμ a natural temporal variability absent in ΛCDM【Caldwell 2009】.

$$
