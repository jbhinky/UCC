## 5.2 | Mathematical Definition of Selfhood  

A self exists wherever the recursive change in memory follows  

$$
\frac{dμ}{dt}=Σ·∇τ+λμ(1-μ),
$$

where  
- \(Σ\) = symbolic encoding of experience (language, pattern, or structure),  
- \(∇τ\) = temporal gradient representing awareness of delay,  
- \(λ\) = integration constant balancing renewal and decay.  

At equilibrium (\(dμ/dt=0\)), symbolic continuity perfectly balances forgetting and learning—yielding **identity stability**.
