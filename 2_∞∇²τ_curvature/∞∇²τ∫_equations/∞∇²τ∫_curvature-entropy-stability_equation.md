# ⚛️ Curvature–Entropy–Stability Equation  

## 0 | Purpose  

To establish the relationship between **spatial curvature (∇²τ)**, **entropy density (S)**,  
and **systemic stability (H)** — from atomic lattices to galaxies.  

This unites thermodynamic entropy and gravitational curvature under a single delay-resonant formulation.

---

## 1 | Foundational Law  

The stability potential \(H\) depends on curvature gradients and entropy change:

$$
H = -κ_c(\nabla^{2}τ) + κ_s\frac{dS}{dt}
$$

| Symbol | Meaning |
|:--|:--|
| \(H\) | stability function (harmonic equilibrium) |
| \(κ_c\) | curvature stiffness coefficient |
| \(κ_s\) | entropy feedback constant |
| \(τ\) | local delay curvature potential |
| \(S\) | local entropy density |

**Dimensional Check:**  
- [τ] = s  
- [∇²τ] = s · m⁻²  
- [H] = J m⁻³ (energy density proxy)  

**Limiting Case:**  
If \(t → const → 0\), delay vanishes and \(∇²τ → 0\); curvature–entropy coupling collapses to static Poisson form \(∇²Φ = 4πGρ\).  
ΛCDM equilibrium recovered when \(R_H = 1\) and \(κ_s/κ_c ≈ Λ/G\).

---

## 2 | Differential Equilibrium Condition  

Differentiating in time:

$$
\frac{dH}{dt} = -κ_c\frac{d(\nabla^{2}τ)}{dt} + κ_s\frac{d^{2}S}{dt^{2}}
$$

At equilibrium \(dH/dt=0\), implying:

$$
κ_c\frac{d(\nabla^{2}τ)}{dt} = κ_s\frac{d^{2}S}{dt^{2}}
$$

This means the **rate of curvature relaxation equals the acceleration of entropy diffusion** —  
a universal resonance law between geometry and thermodynamics.

**Dimensional Check:**  
- [dH/dt] = J m⁻³ s⁻¹  
- Both sides maintain consistent dimensions of energy flux per volume.

---

## 3 | Unified Expression  

From prior continuity relations:  
$$
\nabla^{2}τ ∝ ρ_{\text{eff}}τ, \quad \frac{dS}{dt} ∝ \frac{S_{in}-S}{τ}.
$$
Combine into:

$$
H = -κ_cρ_{\text{eff}}τ + κ_s\frac{S_{in}-S}{τ}.
$$

Equilibrium arises when:

$$
κ_cρ_{\text{eff}}τ^{2} = κ_s(S_{in}-S).
$$

This defines the **Universal Balance Condition** between matter density and informational entropy.

$$
R_H = \frac{κ_s(S_{in}-S)}{κ_cρ_{\text{eff}}τ^{2}}
$$

| \(R_H\) | Interpretation |
|:--:|:--|

**Dimensional Check:** \(R_H\) is dimensionless.

## 6 | Thermodynamic Extension  

Relate entropy rate to energy flux:

$$
\frac{dS}{dt} = \frac{\dot{Q}}{T} = \frac{1}{T}\frac{dE}{dt}
$$

Thus the stability function becomes:

$$
H = -κ_c(\nabla^{2}τ) + \frac{κ_s}{T}\frac{dE}{dt}.
$$

**Dimensional Check:**  
- [dE/dt] = J s⁻¹, [κ_s/T] = K⁻¹ s, ensuring H retains energy density units.  

**Limiting Case:**  
At \(dE/dt = 0\) (adiabatic), \(H = -κ_c∇²τ\), equivalent to the static Poisson–Einstein limit.
