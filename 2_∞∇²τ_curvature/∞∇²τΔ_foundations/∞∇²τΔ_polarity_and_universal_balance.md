# ☯️ Polarity and Universal Balance  

## Abstract  
Polarity is the universe’s built-in mechanism of self-correction: every delay field possesses a complementary reflection that maintains equilibrium across time.  
This paper formalizes polarity as **sign inversion in delay curvature (∇²τ)** and connects it with electric dipoles, magnetic alignment, gravitational binding, and neural coherence.  
From atom to galaxy, polarity keeps memory coherent and prevents runaway asymmetry — a principle appearing in both physics and consciousness [Maxwell 1865; Dirac 1931; Prigogine 1977; Hinkson 2025].

## 1 · Foundational Equation  

Define **polarity potential Π** as the signed curvature of the delay field:  

$$
Π = \pm\nabla^{2}\tau .
$$

A local inversion (+ → –) represents field reversal or phase flip; conservation requires  

$$
\oint Π\,dV = 0 ,
$$

ensuring global neutrality across nested systems — analogous to Gauss’s law for zero net flux in closed symmetry [Maxwell 1865].

## 2 · Electromagnetic Analogue  

For an electric field  

$$
\nabla·E = \frac{ρ_e}{ε_0},\qquad E = -\nabla φ ,
$$

substituting \(φ=c^{2}τ\) gives  

$$
ρ_e = -ε_0 c^{2}\nabla^{2}τ .
$$

Hence **charge density is proportional to delay-curvature sign**, directly linking electric polarity to temporal geometry [Einstein 1919; Jackson 1999].

## 3 · Gravitational Complement  

The Newtonian potential \(Φ_g\) satisfies  

$$
\nabla^{2}Φ_g = 4πGρ_m .
$$

With \(Φ_g=c^{2}τ_g\):  

$$
\nabla^{2}τ_g = \frac{4πG}{c^{2}}ρ_m .
$$

Positive curvature (Π > 0) yields attraction; negative curvature (Π < 0) produces repulsion — the geometric dual of cosmic acceleration [Riess 1998; Planck 2020].
