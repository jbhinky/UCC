# ⚖️ Delay–Field Equilibrium  

## Abstract  
**Delay–Field Equilibrium (DFE)** defines the steady-state condition of the Universal Continuity Continuum where curvature, entropy, and energy achieve recursive balance.  
Equilibrium is reached when the rate of memory formation equals the rate of energy dissipation—yielding  
\(\partial_{t}\tau=0\) and \(\nabla^{2}\tau=\text{constant}\).  
This file establishes the formal equilibrium equation, its boundary conditions, and empirical implications from atomic to cosmological scales.

## 1 · Equilibrium Criterion  
Starting from the unified delay-field equation:
$$
\Box\tau
=\frac{4\pi G}{c^{2}}\rho
-\frac{1}{\hbar}\frac{\partial U_{q}}{\partial t}
+\frac{1}{k_{B}T}\frac{\partial S}{\partial t}
$$

$$
\frac{\partial\tau}{\partial t}=0
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
\nabla^{2}\tau=\frac{4\pi G}{c^{2}}\rho_{\text{eq}}
$$
The field thus reduces to a **Poisson-like curvature balance** where local mass density equals curvature density—defining the static limit of the UCC field.

## 3 · Stability Condition  
Small perturbations \(\delta\tau\) obey:
$$
\frac{\partial^{2}\delta\tau}{\partial t^{2}}
+c^{2}\nabla^{2}\delta\tau
=-\,\omega_{0}^{2}\delta\tau,
\quad
\omega_{0}^{2}=\frac{4\pi G\rho_{\text{eq}}}{c^{2}}
$$
Solutions oscillate with frequency \(\omega_{0}\), implying that the delay field behaves as a **harmonic curvature resonator**.  
Damping or growth indicates departure from equilibrium.

## 4 · Thermodynamic Constraint  
From the delay-thermodynamic law  
$$
P_{\tau}dV_{\tau}=T\,dS+d\mu,
$$
equilibrium implies \(dS/dt=0\) and \(d\mu/dt=0\).  
The system’s total *information energy*  
\(I=\mu V_{\tau}\) is conserved:  
$$
\frac{dI}{dt}=0.
$$
This defines the **isodelay state**—the analog of isothermal equilibrium in classical thermodynamics.
