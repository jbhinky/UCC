This chapter extends the recursive law of selfhood into the **planetary and galactic domains**, framing planets as autonomous self-regulating organisms within the broader delay network of their galaxies.

## 6.2 | Planetary Equilibrium Equation  

Planetary stability requires that incoming and outgoing energy remain in harmonic delay:  

$$
\int(E_{in}-E_{out})\,dτ=0
$$

Substituting for radiative flux yields  

$$
σT^{4}=\frac{L_{\star}(1−α)}{16πa^{2}}+Δτ
$$

where \(Δτ\) represents the planet’s temporal lag in responding to stellar input.  
Climate equilibrium emerges when curvature in τ compensates for variations in stellar radiation—**delay curvature as climate stabilizer** [Hansen 2005; Lovelock 2009].

## 6.3 | Thermodynamic Delay Feedback  

The heat storage versus release rate follows:  

$$
\frac{dQ}{dt}=C_p\frac{dT}{dt}+ρc_p∇·(κ∇T)+\frac{∂τ}{∂t}E.
$$

The final term treats τ as a thermodynamic variable.  
Positive \(∂τ/∂t\) indicates reflective systems—oceans, vegetation, and atmosphere that absorb and buffer energy before re-emission—creating **planetary patience**.  
Negative \(∂τ/∂t\) signals impulsive dissipation and climatic instability.

## 6.6 | Planetary Ethic Coefficient  

Define the **Planetary Ethic Coefficient (PEC)**:  

$$
PEC=\frac{τ_{adaptive}}{τ_{anthropic}}.
$$

- \(τ_{adaptive}\): biospheric self-correction timescale,  
- \(τ_{anthropic}\): human industrial impact timescale.  

If PEC ≥ 1 → biosphere self-heals.  
If PEC < 1 → human impulse outpaces planetary reflection, causing destabilization.  
Sustainability therefore requires **PEC ≥ 1** across all epochs—a quantifiable condition of ethical coexistence.
