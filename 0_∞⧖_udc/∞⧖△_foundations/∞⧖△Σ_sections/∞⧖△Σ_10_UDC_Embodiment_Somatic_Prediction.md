# Section 10: Embodiment and the Somatic Basis of Prediction

UDC describes consciousness as delayed, memory-integrated, recursive awareness.  
This applies not only to **exteroceptive** signals (vision, sound, touch) but also to **interoceptive** signals originating within the body.

Interoception suggests that **internal physiological states**—heart rate, breath rhythm, temperature, hunger, muscular tension—form a hidden predictive layer that continually shapes conscious experience.

The **insula** functions as a major integration hub, coupling bodily state history with affective forecasting. Emotions are thus not merely reactions; they are **predicted somatic simulations**.

## Somatic-Predictive Law (UDC Extension)

UDC somatic prediction can be expressed formally as:

$$
\Sigma_{\text{somatic}}(t)
  = f\Big(
      \mu_{\text{intero}}(t-\tau),\;
      \Delta b(t),\;
      C_{\text{affect}}
    \Big)
$$

| Symbol | Description |
|-------:|-------------|

| `Δb(t)` | Change in bodily signals across delay τ |
| `C_affect` | Affective context shaping interpretation |

## Embodied Cognition and Delay

Evidence from neuroscience and cognitive science shows that:

- **Muscle feedback** alters decision confidence.
- **Gut-brain signaling** influences mood and memory consolidation.
- **Vestibular stability** anchors spatial-temporal self models.
- **Cardiac phase** modulates fear and risk perception.

Thus embodied prediction adheres to UDC’s central claim:

> Awareness is not received, but **constructed across delay**, using internal and external signals.
