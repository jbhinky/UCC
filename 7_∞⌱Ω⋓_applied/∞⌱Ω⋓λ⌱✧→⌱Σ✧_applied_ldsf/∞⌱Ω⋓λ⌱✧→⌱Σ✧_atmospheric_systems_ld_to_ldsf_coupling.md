# Atmospheric Systems ‚Äî LD ‚Üí LDSF Coupling

This document defines the **structural coupling** by which **atmospheric phenomena**, fully defined within **LD (Light Dimensions)**, become classified as a **Light Dimension Shared Field (LDSF)**.

This file:
- introduces **no new physics**
- introduces **no agency or awareness**
- describes **transition of classification**, not causation

## 3. LD Foundations (Pre-Coupling State)

Atmospheric systems originate entirely within LD:

- **LD1** ‚Äî molecular motion and collision dynamics  
- **LD2** ‚Äî gas-phase chemistry and radiative absorption  
- **LD3** ‚Äî thermodynamic gradients and phase transitions  
- **LD4** ‚Äî planetary gravity, rotation, solar forcing, and orbital stability  

At this stage:
- atmospheric processes are **structural**
- interactions are **local**
- no shared-field designation applies

---

## 4. LD5 ‚Äî Planetary Union Operator

The transition from LD to LDSF occurs through **LD5**, defined as:

> **The operator that binds LD4 planetary-scale structure into a globally shared spatial‚Äìtemporal coordinate frame.**

LD5 is **not a dimension**.  
It performs no dynamics.

LD5 establishes:
- common gravity vector
- shared vertical (pressure) gradient
- global horizontal reference (latitude/longitude)
- shared diurnal and seasonal timing

---

## 5. Coupling Condition: Structural to Shared

Atmospheric phenomena qualify as **LDSF** when all of the following are true:

1. **Spatial coherence**  
   Processes extend beyond local interaction and influence planetary-scale regions.

2. **Temporal persistence**  
   Patterns persist across time scales longer than local molecular interactions.

3. **Simultaneity of influence**  
   Multiple independent systems experience the same atmospheric constraints concurrently.

4. **Externality**  
   Processes operate independently of organisms, perception, or interpretation.

When these conditions are met, LD processes become **shared coordination fields**.

## 6. Resulting Classification

After LD5 coupling:

- Atmospheric structure remains governed by LD laws
- Classification shifts to **LDSF1** due to:
  - shared reach
  - external constraint
  - planetary coordination

No new properties are introduced.
