# Civilization ‚Äî LDSF

## 1. Scope and Purpose

This document defines **civilization** as a **Light Dimension Shared Field (LDSF)** within **UCC v2.5.1**.

Here, civilization refers strictly to **externally shared coordination systems created and maintained by human populations**, including:

- infrastructure
- institutions
- economic systems
- governance structures
- settlement patterns
- technological coordination
- shared symbolic standards (without interpretation)

This file does **not** address:
- individual belief
- ethics
- meaning
- identity
- internal experience

## 4. LD Lineage (Structural Parentage)

Civilization arises from LD foundations including:

- **LD1‚ÄìLD2** ‚Äî material resources, energy, tools
- **LD3** ‚Äî thermodynamics of production and transport
- **LD4** ‚Äî planetary stability, geography, climate

Civilization does **not** introduce new physical law.  
It reorganizes physical and biological constraints into **persistent shared systems**.

## 5. Definition of Civilization as LDSF

Within UCC, civilization qualifies as **LDSF** when defined as:

> **Externally observable, shared coordination fields produced by human populations that structure activity, resource flow, communication, and continuity across space and time.**

This includes:
- cities and settlements
- transportation networks
- trade systems
- legal and administrative institutions
- standardized technologies
- shared measurement and record systems

All are **external**, **collective**, and **measurable**.

- governance

### 6.3 Temporal Persistence  
Civilization persists across:
- generations
- regime changes
- demographic turnover

### 6.4 Observability  
Civilizational structures are observable through:
- archaeology
- historical records
- economic data
- infrastructure mapping
- institutional documentation
