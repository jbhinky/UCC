## 5. Definition of Atmospheric Systems (LDSF)

Within UCC, **atmospheric systems** are defined as:

> **Planetary-scale, physically constrained gaseous envelopes whose thermodynamic, chemical, and dynamical processes create shared environmental conditions across spatial and temporal scales.**

These systems:
- distribute energy
- regulate temperature
- transport matter
- establish temporal rhythms (day/night, seasonal cycles)
- constrain habitability

All surface-bound life and human systems operate **within** this field.

Atmospheric systems coordinate multiple shared domains:

- **Energy distribution** (solar radiation, infrared re-emission)
- **Mass transport** (water vapor, aerosols, trace gases)
- **Temporal modulation** (diurnal and seasonal cycles)
- **Boundary regulation** (pressure, temperature gradients)
- **Coupling interfaces** (land‚Äìatmosphere, ocean‚Äìatmosphere)
