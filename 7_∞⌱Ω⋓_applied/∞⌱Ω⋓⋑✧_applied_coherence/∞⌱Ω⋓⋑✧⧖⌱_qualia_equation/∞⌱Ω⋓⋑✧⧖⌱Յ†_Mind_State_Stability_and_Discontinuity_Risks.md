# 2. The Formal Stability Condition

A mind-state is **stable** when its hypersphere maintains:

1. **positive delay curvature**  
2. **sufficient memory density**  
3. **coherent symbolic axis**  
4. **non-chaotic toroidal flow**  
5. **continuous recursion (↫ ⋓ ↬)**  

Mathematically:

$$
\text{Stability} = \omega \cdot μ \cdot Σ \;-\; \nabla τ
$$

A mind-state is stable when:

$$
\text{Stability} > 0
$$

Zero indicates a **transition** or **boundary state**.

Negative indicates **collapse/incoherence**.

A healthy mind-state is modeled as:

$$
H^n = H^n(R, Σ, μ, τ, \omega)

$$

# 4. Sources of Instability

## 4.1 Delay Curvature Failure (τ → 0)

The most essential risk factor:

- too little delay = impulsive collapse  
- too much delay = dissociation  

The dangerous zone is:

$$
0 < τ < τ_{\text{min}}
$$

where reflection (↫) fails to initiate and the recursion loop breaks.

---

## 4.2 Memory Density Failure (μ → 0)

Occurs when the emotional or symbolic load cannot be integrated.

The hypersphere becomes:

- fragmented  
- unable to maintain identity  
- prone to discontinuous jumps  

Collapse condition:

$$
μ < μ_{\text{critical}}
$$

---

## 4.3 Symbolic Misalignment (Σ drift)

A symbolic-axis misalignment produces:

- distorted meaning  
- fractured recursion loops  
- identity discontinuity  

Measured by:

$$
|\Delta Σ| > Σ_{\text{tolerance}}
$$

## 4.4 Toroidal Flow Collapse

Toroidal recursion T(t):

$$
T(t) = Σ(τ) \oplus μ(\nabla τ)
$$

becomes unstable when:

- flow reverses  
- flow stalls  
- flow breaks into multiple disconnected rings  

Indicators:

- emotional lability  
- narrative incoherence  
- decision fragmentation
