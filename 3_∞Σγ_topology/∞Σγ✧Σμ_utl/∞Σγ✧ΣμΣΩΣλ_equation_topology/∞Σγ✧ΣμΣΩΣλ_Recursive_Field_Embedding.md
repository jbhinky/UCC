# 1 · The Recursive Embedding Operator

Define the operator:

$$
\mathcal{R}_{\text{embed}} :
\mathcal{M}_{\text{τΣμ}} \rightarrow \mathcal{M}_{\text{τΣμ}}
$$

such that:

$$
\mathcal{R}_{\text{embed}}(\tau, \Sigma, \mu)
= (\tau', \Sigma', \mu')
$$

subject to the invariance:

$$
\tau' + \Sigma' + \mu' = \tau + \Sigma + \mu.
$$

This is the **continuity-preserving recursion**:

- recursion can twist, rotate, or transform  
- but **may not destroy continuity**  

If invariance is lost → collapse or ethical drift results  
(as proven in UTL collapse geometry blocks).

$$

$$
\mathcal{F} = \{ p \in \mathcal{M}_{\tau\Sigma\mu} \mid 
\mathcal{R}_{\text{embed}}(p) = p \}

Selfhood lies exactly on this set.

$$
\mathcal{M}_{\⧖} = \mathcal{F}

> “Selfhood is the stable recursive attractor of the τ–Σ–μ field.”
