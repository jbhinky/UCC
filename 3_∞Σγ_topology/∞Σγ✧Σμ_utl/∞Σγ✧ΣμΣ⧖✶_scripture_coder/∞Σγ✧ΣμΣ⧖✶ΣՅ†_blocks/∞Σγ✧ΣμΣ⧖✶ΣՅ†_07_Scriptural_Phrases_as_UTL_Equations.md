# ✦ Block 7 — Continuity Under Ethical Drift  
### *How moral framing alters or preserves UTL geometry across languages*

Block 6 mapped continuity across languages.  
**Block 7 introduces ethical drift**, where meaning is altered intentionally or unintentionally:

- through doctrine  
- through political shifts  
- through translation bias  
- through moral overlay  
- through suppression and selective emphasis  

UTL treats ethical drift as a **measurable continuity deviation**, not a theological debate.

---

# 1 · Ethical Drift Geometry — Formal Definition

Define ethical curvature $begin:math:text$ ε $end:math:text$ for any phrase $begin:math:text$ P $end:math:text$ in language $begin:math:text$ L $end:math:text$:

$$
ε(P,L) = \nabla_{\text{moral}} Σ_{P,L}
$$

where moral curvature is the degree to which symbolic meaning is:

- compressed  
- expanded  
- distorted  
- narrowed  
- moralized  
- weaponized  

Total continuity under ethical pressure is:

$$
\mathcal{C}_{ε}(P,L) =
\left\| \nabla_{(τ, Σ + ε, μ)} \Psi_{P,L} \right\|
$$

Drift is defined as:

$$
\Delta_{ε}(P; L_1 \to L_2) =
\left\| \mathcal{C}_{ε}(P,L_1) - \mathcal{C}_{ε}(P,L_2) \right\|.
$$

Small drift → ethical neutrality.  
Large drift → doctrinal overlay.

Ethical drift is an **early warning sign** of:

- civilizational collapse  
- semantic entropy  
- doctrinal distortion  
- loss of shared meaning  
- breakdown of continuity  

This aligns with UCC’s global continuity criteria:

$$
\mathcal{S}_{\text{civilization}}
=
1 - \frac{\Delta_{\text{drift}}^{\max}}{\mathcal{C}_{\text{root}}+\epsilon}
$$

Societies with high ethical drift experience:

- political fragmentation  
- religious extremism  
- collapse of trust  
- semantic warfare  
- history rewriting  

Societies with low ethical drift maintain:

- coherence  
- shared identity  
- stable ethics  
- stable language  
- stable memory
