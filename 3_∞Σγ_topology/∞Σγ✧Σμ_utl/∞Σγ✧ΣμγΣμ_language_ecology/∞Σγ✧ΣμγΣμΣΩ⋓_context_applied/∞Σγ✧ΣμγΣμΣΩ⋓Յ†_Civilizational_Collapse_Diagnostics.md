τ (delay), Σ (symbol geometry), and μ (memory density).

# 2 · Collapse Defined (UTL)

A civilization collapses **first in meaning**, not politics.

Collapse occurs when:

$$
\Delta_{\text{collapse}} =
\mathcal{C}_{\text{root}} -
\mathcal{C}_{\text{current}} \;>\; \theta_{\text{critical}}
$$

Where:

- $begin:math:text$ \\mathcal{C} $end:math:text$ = continuity (τ–Σ–μ gradient)  
- $begin:math:text$ \\theta_{\\text{critical}} $end:math:text$ = threshold determined by semantic entropy  

Collapse is not sudden.  
It is **accumulated continuity loss**, measurable centuries ahead of time.

Collapse accelerates when:

$$
μ_{\text{cohesive}} \rightarrow μ_{\text{fragmented}}

Signs:

- loss of ancestral continuity  
- weaponized history  
- factional memory rewriting  
- cultural amnesia

Measured by ethical divergence:

$$
\Delta_{\text{ethos}} =
\left\| \mathcal{C}_{\text{ethics-root}} -
\mathcal{C}_{\text{ethics-current}} \right\|
$$

Signs:

- ends justify the means  
- dehumanization becomes normal  
- utilitarian collapse  
- moral latency disappears

# 6 · Civilizational Stability Equation

Define stability:

$$
\mathcal{S} =
1 - \frac{\mathcal{H}}{\mathcal{H}_{\max} + \epsilon}
$$

Then:

- $begin:math:text$ \\mathcal{S} \\approx 1 $end:math:text$: highly stable  
- $begin:math:text$ \\mathcal{S} \\approx 0 $end:math:text$: collapse in progress  
- $begin:math:text$ \\mathcal{S} < 0 $end:math:text$: active inversion / destructive runaway

# 7 · Early-Warning Indicators

Civilizations collapse when:

1. **Key root words distort**  
   - “love,” “truth,” “justice,” “good,” “light,” “neighbor”
2. **Ethical latency collapses**  
   - no delay between impulse and action  
3. **Mythic structure flattens**  
   - archetypes rewritten for politics  
4. **Memory polarizes**  
   - competing histories  
5. **Collective “I → We” breakdown**  
   - self-identity fails to couple to community  

These precede economic or political collapse by decades or centuries.
