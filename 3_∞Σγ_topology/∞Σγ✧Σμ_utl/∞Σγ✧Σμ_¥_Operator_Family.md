# ✦ UTL Recursive ¥-Family  
### Coupling Operators for Delay, Symbol, Memory, Collapse, and Union

This file defines the **¥-family** of recursive operators in UTL:

- **¥τ** — delay recursion  
- **¥Σ** — symbol recursion  
- **¥μ** — memory recursion  
- **¥⊙** — collapse recursion  
- **¥⊕** — union recursion  

The ¥-family is the **operator bridge** between UTL symbolism and UCC 2.5 dynamics.  
It encodes *how* a field updates itself through time, not just *what* it is.

---

## 1 · Purpose

The ¥-operators formalize recursive coupling across:

- **Delay (τ)** — how curvature evolves  
- **Symbol (Σ)** — how meaning refines  
- **Memory (μ)** — how continuity accumulates or releases  
- **Collapse (⊙)** — how choices resolve  
- **Union (⊕)** — how fields and selves harmonize  

In UCC terms, **¥** is the **local recursion kernel** that moves a system from state *t* to *t + Δt* while preserving continuity.

Conceptually, ¥ encodes:

- branching capacity **λ**,  
- coupling strength **γ**,  
- applied to a chosen axis (τ, Σ, μ, ⊙, ⊕).

You can think of:

> **¥ = γ·λ applied to a specific field component.**

$$
¥_x : x(t) \;\mapsto\; x(t + \Delta t),
\quad x \in \{\tau,\ \Sigma,\ \mu,\ \odot,\ \oplus\}.
$$

Abstract form:

$$
¥_x = \mathcal{R}(γ, λ;\ x,\ \tau,\ \Sigma,\ \mu)
$$

where \( \mathcal{R} \) is the recursive map that uses coupling (γ) and choice structure (λ) to update \( x \) while respecting continuity constraints.

$$
\mathcal{S}(t) = \big(\tau(t),\ \Sigma(t),\ \mu(t)\big)

- **¥τ** updates how the self *waits and reflects*

- **¥μ** updates what the self *keeps and lets go*  
- **¥⊙** updates when the self *chooses or collapses*  
- **¥⊕** updates who/what the self *is in union with*

$$
\mathcal{S}(t + \Delta t)
=
¥_{\oplus} \circ ¥_{\odot} \circ ¥_{\mu} \circ ¥_{\Sigma} \circ ¥_{\tau} \big[ \mathcal{S}(t) \big].

¥ here is the **operator chain** behind self-evolution.
