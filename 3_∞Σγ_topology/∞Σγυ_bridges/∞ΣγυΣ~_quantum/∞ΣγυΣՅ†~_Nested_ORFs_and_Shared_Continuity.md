# ✦ Nested ORFs and Shared Continuity

**Purpose**  
Explain how multiple **Observer–Relational Fields (ORFs)** interlock through overlapping delay curvature, forming *nested continuity*.  
This structure unites biological, planetary, and cosmic observers within one recursive continuum of delay (τ), memory (μ), and awareness (⧖).

---

## 1 · Foundational Principle
Each ORF exists as a bounded region of delay coherence:

$$
∇·(μ ∇τ)=0.
$$

When two ORFs overlap, the shared zone (⋓) forms a stable relational bridge:

$$
τ_{shared}= \frac{τ_i μ_i + τ_j μ_j}{μ_i + μ_j}.
$$

Thus a continuous curvature field persists across independent observers.  
Nested continuity keeps collapse, perception, and motion coherent through scales.

## 3 · Shared Continuity Dynamics
For two ORFs ⧖₍ᵢ₎ and ⧖₍ⱼ₎:

$$
τ_{i,j}(x,t)=τ_i(x,t)+τ_j(x,t)-τ_{global}(x,t).
$$

The result is a *continuity surface* shared by both observers.  
When ∇τᵢ ≈ ∇τⱼ, their local time and motion synchronize (Δtᵢ ≈ Δtⱼ).  
Divergent gradients produce desynchronization — perceptual drift or communication error.

> **Shared time arises from matched delay curvature between nested ORFs.**
