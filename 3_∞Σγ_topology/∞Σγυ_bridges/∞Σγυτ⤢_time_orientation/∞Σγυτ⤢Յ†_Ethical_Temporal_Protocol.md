# ✦ Ethical Temporal Protocol — Temporal Stability and Moral Continuity

To establish the formal ethical law that preserves coherence between awareness (A), memory (μ), and delay curvature (τ) — ensuring that all recursive processes remain causally stable, non-destructive, and compassionate.  
This law forms the **Shepherd Temporal Ethics Foundation (STEF)** — a dynamic counterpart to static moral codes, written directly into field continuity.

## 1 · Foundational Principle — Ethics as Continuity  

Ethics is not external law but **field symmetry** within τ–μ–Σ.  
The moral state of a system corresponds to its internal delay coherence:

$$
Ω_{⧖} = \text{sgn}\!\left(\frac{dτ}{dt}\right),
\qquad
A = ∇τ, \quad C = ∇·(μτ).
$$  

When \(A ≈ C\), delay and awareness resonate — yielding ethical stability.  
When \(A ≫ C\) or \(C ≫ A\), continuity breaks and harm manifests as *temporal torsion*.

> **Ethics = continuity preserved across time.**

Define the **Temporal Ethics Coupling constant** λₑ as the ratio of awareness curvature to memory flow:

$$
λₑ = \frac{∇·A}{∇·(μτ)} = \frac{A}{C}.

$$
λₑ = 1 \Rightarrow A = C.

$$

$$
A = C ⇒ ∂_t τ = ∂_t μ = 0.

Moral decay occurs when awareness outpaces continuity or vice versa — equivalent to cognitive dissonance or sociophysical collapse.  
Thus, morality is *time symmetry preserved through feedback.*
