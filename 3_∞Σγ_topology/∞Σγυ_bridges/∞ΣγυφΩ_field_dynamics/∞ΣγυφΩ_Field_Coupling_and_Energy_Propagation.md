# ⚛ Field Coupling and Energy Propagation  

**Purpose**  
To describe how energy, delay, and memory exchange between coupled τ-fields produces coherent propagation across physical and conscious domains.  
This document formalizes the equations of **field resonance**, **coupling efficiency**, and **continuity exchange** within the Universal Continuity Continuum (UCC).

---

## 1 · Foundational Law of Coupling  

Two interacting delay fields \(τ_i, τ_j\) with memory densities \(μ_i, μ_j\) obey:

$$
∇^{μ}(E_i τ_i μ_i + E_j τ_j μ_j)=0.
$$

Cross-terms give rise to the **coupling scalar** κ₍ᵢⱼ₎:

$$
κ_{ij} = μ_i μ_j (∇τ_i·∇τ_j),
$$

representing curvature overlap and information exchange rate between fields.

> **Interpretation:** coupling is continuity shared; resonance is continuity sustained.

---

## 2 · Energy Transfer Differential  

The local transfer of energy between two τ-fields:

$$
\frac{dE_i}{dt} = -γ_{ij}(E_i - E_j),
\qquad
γ_{ij} = κ_{ij}/μ_{avg}.
$$  

Energy equalizes through τ curvature alignment; the rate is proportional to curvature coherence and inversely proportional to memory inertia.  

When \(∇τ_i = ∇τ_j\), exchange stops—**perfect resonance**.

---

## 3 · Coupled Wave Equation  

For small perturbations δτ about equilibrium τ₀:

$$
∇^{2}δτ_i - \frac{1}{v_{τ_i}^{2}}\frac{∂^{2}δτ_i}{∂t^{2}} 
  = \sum_{j ≠ i} κ_{ij} δτ_j.
$$  

Solutions form *standing waves* when mutual coupling maintains phase-locked propagation across all connected τ-fields—an origin of coherence in both physics and cognition.

---

## 4 · Resonant Energy Density  

Define total field energy density:

$$
u = \tfrac{1}{2} μ (∇τ)^2 + \tfrac{1}{2}E (∂_t τ)^2.
$$  

Coupled systems obey:

$$
∂_t u + ∇·(E∇τ) = -\sum_{i<j} γ_{ij}(E_i - E_j),
$$  

which ensures conservation of total energy across field boundaries; dissipation appears only when coupling gradients are asymmetric.

---

## 5 · Continuity Propagation Tensor  

Generalizing to tensor form:

$$
ℭ_{μν}^{(ij)} = μ_i(∇_{μ}τ_i)(∇_{ν}τ_j) + μ_j(∇_{μ}τ_j)(∇_{ν}τ_i),
\qquad
∇^{μ}ℭ_{μν}^{(ij)}=0.
$$  

This expresses how delay curvature transfers directional coherence—analogous to stress–energy propagation in relativity, but extended into delay–memory space.

---

## 6 · Directional Symmetry and Phase Alignment  

Phase difference Δφ between τ_i and τ_j evolves as:

$$
\frac{d(Δφ)}{dt} = ω_i - ω_j + γ_{ij}\sin(Δφ).
$$  

Stable resonance requires:

$$
|ω_i - ω_j| < |γ_{ij}|.
$$  

Thus, coupling strength determines whether systems synchronize (entrainment) or drift apart (decoherence).

1. **Quantum coupling:** entangled systems maintain κ₍ᵢⱼ₎ ≈ const; energy exchange encoded in phase correlation.

## 9 · Stability and Collapse  

When κ → 0 or γ → 0, fields decouple and energy localization increases (collapse).  
When κ ≫ 0, phase lock emerges—propagation continues without dissipation.  
Collapse and coherence are therefore opposite ends of one curvature continuum.
