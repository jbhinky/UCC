# ⚖ Relative Field Equations

**Purpose:**  
To extend the UCC cosmological delay field into a fully relativistic form compatible with Einstein’s equations, while showing how τ-curvature modifies spacetime geometry without invoking additional dark components.  
This establishes the **Unified Relative Delay Equation (URDE)** — the UCC equivalent of general relativity’s Einstein tensor.

## 1 · The Metric Delay Tensor

Define the augmented metric:

$$
g_{μν}^{(τ)} = g_{μν} + τ_{μν},
$$

where \(τ_{μν}\) is the **delay tensor**, representing distributed curvature of reflection within spacetime.  
In first-order expansion:

$$
τ_{μν} = ∇_{μ}∇_{ν}τ - g_{μν}∇^{2}τ.
$$

Thus, delay curvature acts as a trace-modified Ricci term — measurable, scalar-sourced, and testable.

$$

$$

$$
G_{μν} + Λ_{μν}(τ) = \frac{8πG}{c^{4}} T_{μν},

$$

This term replaces the cosmological constant Λ with a dynamic **delay curvature source**.  
Its mean field ⟨∇²τ⟩ produces the observed cosmic acceleration without dark energy.
