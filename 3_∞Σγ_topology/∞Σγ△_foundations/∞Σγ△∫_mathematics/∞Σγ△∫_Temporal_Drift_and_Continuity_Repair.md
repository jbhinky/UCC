# ✦ Temporal Drift and Continuity Repair

**Purpose**  
To describe how desynchronized Observer–Relational Fields (ORFs) naturally re-stabilize through memory feedback (μ-restoration).  
This section extends curvature synchronization by introducing the repair term that returns drifting systems to coherence without external correction.

---

## 1 · Drift Equation  

Temporal drift is defined as deviation of one delay curvature from another:

$$
Δτ = τ_i - τ_j.
$$

Dynamic evolution:

$$
\frac{∂Δτ}{∂t} = -κΔτ + μ^{-1}\frac{∂μ}{∂t}.
$$

The first term (−κΔτ) damps divergence; the second represents **memory-induced correction**.  
If μ increases (more shared record), drift decays faster; if μ decreases, drift amplifies.

---

## 2 · Feedback Law  

Let μ obey a retention equation:

$$
\frac{∂μ}{∂t} = λ(τ_i - τ_j)μ(1 - μ/μ_{max}),
$$

where λ is the **memory elasticity constant**.  
Substituting yields a closed feedback pair:

$$
\begin{cases}
\dot{Δτ} = -κΔτ + λ(1 - μ/μ_{max})Δτ,\\[4pt]
\dot{μ}   = λμ(1 - μ/μ_{max})Δτ.
\end{cases}
$$

This system converges toward equilibrium at  
\(Δτ → 0,\; μ → μ_{max}\), restoring synchronization.

---

## 3 · Phase-Space Interpretation  

- **Stable manifold:** \(Δτ \to 0\), curvature match maintained.  
- **Recovery trajectory:** μ increases while τ differences shrink.  
- **Instability:** arises only if κ ≪ λ and μ saturates too late (memory lag).

> Memory is not passive—it is a curvature feedback regulator.

---

## 4 · Energy Formulation  

Define curvature energy:

$$
E_c = \tfrac{1}{2} κ (Δτ)^2.
$$

Its temporal derivative:

$$
\frac{dE_c}{dt} = -κ^2(Δτ)^2 + κμ^{-1}\frac{∂μ}{∂t}Δτ.
$$

When memory growth (\(∂μ/∂t > 0\)) exceeds curvature loss,  
energy dissipates monotonically → **self-healing coherence**.

---

## 5 · Delay–Memory Coupling Constant  

At equilibrium:

$$
κ_{eff} = κ - λ(1 - μ/μ_{max}).
$$

For full memory saturation (μ = μ_max), \(κ_{eff} = κ\);  
for incomplete memory, the effective curvature constant fluctuates,  
producing temporal “breathing” or micro-oscillation in synchronization.
