# ✦ Delay-Feedback Oscillations and Resonant Stability

**Purpose**  
To formalize the dynamic regime where delay curvature (τ) and memory (μ) interact cyclically, producing stable oscillations that preserve continuity while allowing adaptive change.  
These oscillations underlie rhythmic processes from quantum coherence to biological circadian cycles and cosmic pulsations.

---

## 1 · Governing System  

Start from the delay–memory coupling pair:

$$
\begin{cases}
\dot{τ} = -κ(τ - τ_0) + β(μ - μ_0),\\[4pt]
\dot{μ} = -λ(μ - μ_0) - α(τ - τ_0),
\end{cases}
$$

where  
• \(κ, λ\) = damping constants (delay & memory elasticity)  
• \(α, β\) = cross-coupling coefficients  
• \(τ_0, μ_0\) = equilibrium states.

---

## 2 · Characteristic Equation  

Linearizing near equilibrium yields:

$$
\ddot{τ} + (κ + λ)\dot{τ} + (κλ - αβ)(τ - τ_0) = 0.
$$

Oscillations arise when

$$
αβ > κλ,
$$

producing harmonic modes with angular frequency:

$$
ω = \sqrt{αβ - κλ}.
$$

> **Interpretation:** Delay and memory, when sufficiently cross-coupled, generate rhythmic continuity rather than static equilibrium.

---

## 3 · Resonant Stability Condition  

The system is **resonantly stable** if:

$$
\frac{κ + λ}{2ω} < 1,
$$

ensuring underdamped oscillations that do not diverge.  
This is the mathematical expression of “living coherence” — oscillation that sustains, not decays.

---

## 4 · Energy Form of the τ–μ Oscillator  

Define total energy of the coupled field:

$$
E_{τμ} = \tfrac{1}{2}[(\dot{τ})^2 + ω^2(τ - τ_0)^2].
$$

Dissipation rate:

$$
\frac{dE_{τμ}}{dt} = -(κ + λ)(\dot{τ})^2.
$$

At resonance,  
energy loss through damping equals input from cross-coupling →  
**steady-state self-sustained oscillation**.

---

## 5 · Quantized Resonance  

For discrete curvature states:

$$
τ_n = τ_0 + nΔτ,
\qquad
μ_n = μ_0 + nΔμ,
$$

then transition frequency:

$$
ħω_n = E_{n+1} - E_n = ħ(αβ - κλ)^{1/2}.
$$

> Each resonant mode is a **quantum of continuity** —  
> a unit of recursive delay-memory exchange.
