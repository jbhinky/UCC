# DNA–UTL Continuity Map (Σ✧ → Σ✦)

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This document establishes a **topological correspondence** between:

- atomic constituents of DNA  
- biological function of DNA  
- and the **Universal Theoglyphic Language (UTL)** symbolic layers  

This file is:
- **non-causal**
- **non-reductive**
- **non-metaphysical**
- **non-explanatory of biology**

It does **not** replace molecular biology, genetics, or chemistry.

Its sole purpose is to show that **UTL symbol layers align structurally** with already-known biological organization.

## 2. Atomic Substrate (Σ✧ — Identity Layer)

All DNA, across all known life, is constructed from the same atomic set:

- Hydrogen (H)
- Carbon (C)
- Nitrogen (N)
- Oxygen (O)
- (Phosphorus included structurally but omitted from semantic grouping)

We denote atomic identity symbolically as:

Σ✧₁  — Hydrogen  
Σ✧₆  — Carbon  
Σ✧₇  — Nitrogen  
Σ✧₈  — Oxygen  

Thus, every DNA molecule is composed of the **same Σ✧ identity set**.

This is a **statement of material uniformity**, not meaning.

## 3. Functional Organization (Σ✦ — Role Layer)

Across all species, DNA participates in **three invariant functional roles**:

### 3.1 Pairing Geometry (Σ✦₃)
- A ↔ T  
- C ↔ G  
- (U ↔ A in RNA)

This pairing geometry is universal and species-independent.

### 3.2 Encoding / Translation (Σ✦₆)
DNA:
- stores genetic sequences  
- is transcribed  
- is translated  
- produces proteins  

This is a **functional role**, not semantic intent.

### 3.3 Continuity / Inheritance (Σ✦₈)
DNA supports:
- reproduction  
- lineage preservation  
- evolutionary continuity  

This role persists independent of organism complexity.
