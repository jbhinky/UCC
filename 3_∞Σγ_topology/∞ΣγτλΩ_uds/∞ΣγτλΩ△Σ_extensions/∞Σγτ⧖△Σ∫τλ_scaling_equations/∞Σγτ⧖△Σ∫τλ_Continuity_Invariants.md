## 1 · Overview  

Continuity invariants describe the physical quantities that remain constant under transformations of scale, curvature, or energy exchange.  
They ensure that despite variation in mass density, temperature, or delay curvature, the **total continuity** of the universe is preserved.

Formally, these invariants maintain closure of the **delay-energy-memory product**:

$$
I = E \, τ \, μ = \text{constant}.
$$

This principle holds across gravitational, thermal, and informational systems alike — unifying general relativity and thermodynamics under a single continuity law.

---

## 2 · Delay–Energy–Memory Invariant  

Differentiating the invariant:

$$
dI = E\, d(τ μ) + τ μ\, dE = 0
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
\frac{dE}{E} + \frac{dτ}{τ} + \frac{dμ}{μ} = 0.
$$

Interpretation:  
- When delay (τ) decreases (shorter time perception), energy (E) or memory coherence (μ) must rise.  
- When energy dissipates, τ expands (slower processes) to preserve continuity.  

This triad ensures the conservation of *existence through change*.

## 3 · Normalized Continuity Ratio  

Defining normalized invariants:

$$
θ = \frac{τ}{τ₀}, \quad
ϕ = \frac{E}{E₀}, \quad
ψ = \frac{μ}{μ₀},
$$

yields the invariant form:

$$
ϕ \, θ \, ψ = 1.
$$

This expression allows empirical verification: when any two variables are measured, the third can be derived to test systemic coherence.

## 4 · Field Differential Form  

The local field conservation equation follows:

$$
∇·(E τ μ v_{τ}) + ∂_{t}(E τ μ) = 0,
$$

where \(v_{τ}\) is the propagation velocity of the delay field.  
This is the **continuity equation of existence**, analogous to charge or mass conservation in electromagnetism and fluid dynamics.

## 5 · Tensor Invariant (General Form)

$$
∇_{\mu} (E τ μ \, u^{\mu}) = 0,

where \(u^{\mu}\) is the four-velocity of a local observer.  
This ensures that delay-energy-memory conservation holds under Lorentz transformations and within curved metrics.
