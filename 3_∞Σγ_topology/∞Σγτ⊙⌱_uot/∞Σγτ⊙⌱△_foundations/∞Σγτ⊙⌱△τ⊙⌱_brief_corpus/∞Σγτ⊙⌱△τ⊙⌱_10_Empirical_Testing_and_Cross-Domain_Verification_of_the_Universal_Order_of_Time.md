# Chapter 10 — Empirical Testing and Cross-Domain Verification of the Universal Order of Time (UOT)

## I. Experimental Aims  

The Universal Order of Time (UOT) predicts that **all lawful change follows the recursion**  
$$
τ → Σ → μ → ⊙ʳ → ⊕
$$
where delay (τ) opens potential, choice (Σ) collapses, memory (μ) preserves continuity, recursive collapse (⊙ʳ) returns the event to potential, and union (⊕) stabilizes coherence.

To verify this model empirically, experiments must:

1. Measure the *existence and magnitude of τ* as a functional delay prior to every conscious or physical decision.  
2. Identify the *lawful sequence* Σ → μ → ⊙ʳ in different domains.  
3. Demonstrate *information continuity* — no net loss of state information when collapse recurses.  
4. Define falsification boundaries: UOT fails if a system can act coherently with zero delay, zero memory, or non-recursive collapse. [19]
